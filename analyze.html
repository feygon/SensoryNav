Local Analysis

Drop in a capture — a .wav and its .json sidecar — to analyze it. This runs entirely in your browser; nothing is uploaded. The audio is scored on your device and then discarded; only the derived, deidentified features remain.

Glossary

…% of windows pinned at roughness 100ⓘ

spectral flatness — noise-like vs tone-likeⓘ

Per-band spectral-chaos ribbon

Chaos (spectral flatness, tonal→noise) for sub-bass · low · mid · high, all on one shared dB scale so the bands are directly comparable. Sub-bass carries the strongest chaos signal; low/mid/high chaos appears only here. Ticks mark the same tagged events as the timeline — hover near one for its tags.

Both files come from the recorder. The audio is decoded and scored on your device (in a background worker) into the same stacked timeline as a scored research pass — speed, roughness, folded sub-bass, band panels, and tagged acoustic events. The audio is never saved or uploaded.
