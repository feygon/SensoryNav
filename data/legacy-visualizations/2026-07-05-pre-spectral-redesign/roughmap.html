The road is drawn by the GPS cells themselves (north up). Each dot is a ~25m stretch, colored by its median roughness across the passes that drove it (blue = calm → yellow = rough), sized by how many of the 5 passes covered it. A solid red ring marks a consistent rough spot (≥3 passes agree). A dashed orange ring marks a worst-chop spot the consensus misses — a pass hit serious chop there (peak > 6.6 dB) but usually only the pass that crawled slowly enough sensed it (e.g. the low-speed turnoff); those dots are colored by their peak, not median. Reproducibility: ICC 0.63 (63% of roughness variance is explained by location, not pass-to-pass noise); split-half agreement ρ 0.61. Talking windows are excluded; each pass scored on its own baseline.
